    Broken Altar

    The name Broken Altar is inspired by The Altar a poem by 17th-century Anglican priest George Herbert. In it, Herbert presents his heart as an altar that only God can make holy:

    'A broken altar, Lord, thy servant rears,
    Made of a heart and cemented with tears.'

    These words express our central idea: That God does not require perfect offerings, but welcomes hearts surrendered to him in prayer.
